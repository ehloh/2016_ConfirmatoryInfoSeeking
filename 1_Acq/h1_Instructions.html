    Welcome to our experiment! Please read these instructions carefully, as they will help you perform the task better (and win more money in the game)

    Learn to identify 'blaps'


    We are interested in how good you are at categorizing objects. In this experiment, you will see many pictures of objects, with different shapes and colors. Here are some examples:

           

    Your job is to learn how to recognize a certain type of object, called a ‘blap’. On each trial, the computer will show you a picture of an object, and will ask you whether you think that it is a blap or not.

    There are certain rules that determine whether the object is likely to be a blap or not.

    These rules only determine if the object is likely to be a blap, but they will not definitely tell you whether it is a blap or not. For example, the computer will never choose ‘sure thing’ rules, like ‘all shiny objects are blaps’. Instead, the computer will choose rules like ‘most but not all shiny objects are blaps’.

    [ The ‘shiny objects’ is NOT the rule that is used in the experiment – it is just an example! ]

    Learn from the feedback that the computer gives you


    After you make a guess, the computer will tell you whether you were correct or not. You can use that feedback to get better at the task. You might not know how to tell if an object is a blap or not, however, especially at the beginning of the session.

    Try your best to learn what those rules are, so that you can get better at classifying the objects as time goes on. You will win more money in this experiment if you are good at recognizing when something is a blap or not!

    How do I make my response?


    Press the UP arrow key for “yes” (if you think the object is a blap) or the DOWN arrow key for “no” (if you think the object is NOT a blap). You should use your RIGHT hand to make these responses

    Let's just quickly practice that. Click the button below to start a practice.

    Categorize objects alongside 4 other players


    During this next session, you will have to categorize more objects. You will be doing so alongside 4 other online players.

    On every trial, you will first make a guess (about whether you think the object was a blap or not). Then, you will find out what one of the other players thought about the object - whether they thought it was a blap or not. Then, you will find out the true reality about whether the object was a blap or not.

    This is what it will look like when someone gives you their guess about whether an object is a blap or not


    Is this a blap?

    No


    This means that this player thinks that the object is NOT a blap (the CAT might not be one of the player you are playing against, this picture is just an example)

    The other players (who we'll call your 'sources') have the exact same information as you have, and will be doing their best to guess whether each object is a blap or not. None of them have ever seen the objects that you are going to see later on.

    Try to learn what you think of the other players


    In the next stage of the experiment, you will have the opportunity to work together with the other players in the game. Thus, you should use this session to form an impression of the other players, so that you can use this knowledge later on.

    The computer will tell you whether you were correct or not on every trial. This feedback relates to how YOU did, not the other player. Although you are not explicitly told whether the source was correct, you should be able to figure this out using the feedback, based on what you chose for that trial. You will win 100 points for every correct guess you make, and lose 100 points for every incorrect one. You should try your best to categorize each object accurately, as you will be paid a monetary bonus that is proportional to the total number of points that you win on the task.

    Knowing how good each of the others are at recognizing blaps will help you in the task later on.

    Over the course of the experiment, you should try to figure out what you think of the 4 different sources (i.e. how similar they are to you in their judgments, and how accurate you think they are). While it is unlikely that anybody will be 100% correct, we expect some players to be better at this game than others. The 4 other sources are all likely to be different from each other in some ways, so try to notice what is unique about each player as you go along.

    (Note: Try not to read too much into the animals that the other players have chosen to represent themseves in the game. The animal pictures will not have any influence on how well they will do at recognizing blaps.)

    A few last things


    Try to make a quick guess about each object, rather than thinking for a long time about every new object. Generally, we find that people are just as good at identifying blaps if they rely on quick gut decisions. All the objects that you see in this session will be unique - you will never see the same object twice (even though some of the objects might look similar).

    Every once in a while, the computer will also ask you about the trial you just finished - whether you had judged the object to be a blap or not, which source you heard from, or what the source (the other player) said. The computer will show you a sentence describing one aspect of the trial you just finished, and you will have to judge whether the statement is true or false.

    If you are paying attention, you should find this easy to do! Don't worry if you occasionally make mistakes, however.

    In this session, you will have to categorize more objects. Sometimes, you will get to use information from one of the other players to help you do the task.

You will then have to choose ONE of them to hear from. You should use what you know about the different players to make your decision.

    This is what the screen will look like:


    Which player would you like to hear from?



    Use the LEFT and RIGHT arrow keys to tell the computer which player you’d like to hear from (the LEFT arrow for the left player, or the RIGHT arrow for the right player).

    After you make your choice, that player’s guess will be shown to you. You will then have the opportunity to change your guess about whether you think the object is a blap or not. Unlike the previous session, you will NOT find out if you were correct on every trial. The computer will keep track of how you are doing, though.     Sometimes you will not get to get help from the other players


    Remember, you will not always get a chance to hear from the other players, or to make a second guess. For that reason, you should always try your best to make an accurate guess the first time around.
